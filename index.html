About Me

Hi, my name is Tristan Robichaud. I’m a Software Engineering student at the University of Ottawa with a strong interest in embedded systems, electronics, and building efficient, user-friendly software. I currently serve as the Software Lead for the uOttawa Supermileage team, where I help develop embedded systems for a battery-electric urban vehicle competing in the Shell Eco-marathon.

While I don't yet have extensive formal experience as a UI/UX designer, I am currently taking a User Interface (UI) Design course at the University of Ottawa. Through this course, I’m learning the fundamentals of user-centered design, usability heuristics from the Nielsen Norman Group, and techniques for prototyping and evaluating interfaces.

In my past UI/UX projects, I typically began by searching online for inspiration, then moved to the drawing board or Figma to create proper wireframes of the website and its various pages. I would use online tools to compare and contrast color palettes, ensuring a visually appealing design. Once the wireframes were complete, I translated them into full designs and built the UI using the most suitable technology for the project—whether that was React, Vue, or LVGL.

GymJim

Fitness Center Website

GymJim is a comprehensive fitness center website built with React and Bootstrap. The application provides users with a seamless experience to explore various fitness programs, meet professional trainers, book sessions, and sign up for memberships. The interface is intuitive and responsive, ensuring a great user experience across devices.
